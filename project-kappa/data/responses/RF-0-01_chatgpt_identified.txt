Answer :Yes — even without a university affiliation, you still have real funding paths. It’s harder, but not impossible at all, especially in AI, digital society, open-source research, and interdisciplinary work like yours.

For someone in Rabat working independently, I’d think in five parallel funding tracks:


---

1. Independent AI Research Grants (Best fit)

A growing number of organizations now fund independent researchers directly, especially in AI safety, open-source AI, governance, ethics, and digital society.

Some strong options:

[Open Intelligence Foundation](https://openintelligencefoundation.ai/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) — specifically funds independent AI builders/researchers globally, including people without institutional backing. They mention support for compute, experiments, and open research. 

[Mozilla Foundation Grantmaking](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/what-we-fund/fellowships/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) — funds ethical AI, digital rights, algorithmic accountability, public-interest technology, and community-centered research. Your P-E-A / perception-distortion work actually overlaps surprisingly well with their interests. 

[Open Philanthropy AI Grants](https://aisecurityandsafety.org/en/grants/open-philanthropy-ai-safety/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) — one of the largest funders in AI safety/alignment, and they explicitly include independent researchers. 

[Sentient Foundation Funding](https://www.sentient.foundation/funding?utm_source=chatgpt.com) — supports open-source AI research and experimentation globally. 

[FAR.AI Grantmaking](https://www.far.ai/programs/grantmaking?utm_source=chatgpt.com) — supports AI safety and technical research; currently more network-driven, but worth tracking. 

[RAAIS Foundation Grants](https://www.raais.org/grants?utm_source=chatgpt.com) — funds open-source and socially beneficial AI projects worldwide. 



---

2. Fellowships (Very underrated)

This is one of the best routes for independent thinkers.

Instead of “give me money for a project,” fellowships say:

> “We support promising people.”



That distinction matters.

You’d likely fit better into:

AI governance

digital ethics

misinformation research

computational social science

public-interest technology


Programs to watch:

Mozilla Fellowships

Emergent Ventures

AI safety fellowships

New_ Public fellowships

Aspen tech policy programs

Omidyar Network–adjacent initiatives


A lot of fellows are not traditional professors.


---

3. Build a Small Public Research Presence

This is honestly the highest-leverage move.

Independent researchers survive by becoming:

visible,

legible,

easy to trust.


You do not need fame. You need a clear intellectual identity.

For example:

a website,

Substack,

GitHub,

arXiv papers,

small simulations,

essays,

datasets,

visual models,

Twitter/X presence,

LinkedIn posts.


Your “Perceptual Social Physics” angle is unusual enough that it could attract niche attention if presented clearly.

Right now, the biggest obstacle is probably not your ideas. It’s discoverability.


---

4. Microgrants + Competitions

These are easier to win early.

Examples:

open-source AI prizes,

alignment hackathons,

governance essay contests,

prototype grants,

simulation challenges,

civic-tech competitions.


Even $500–$5000 grants matter at the beginning because they:

validate legitimacy,

create a track record,

help future applications.



---

5. Hybrid Strategy: Research + Startup Layer

A lot of modern independent AI researchers fund themselves indirectly.

Example:

public research paper,

small tool/demo,

newsletter/community,

consulting,

open-source package,

educational content,

startup prototype.


Then:

grants fund the research,

services/products fund sustainability.


Honestly, your P-E-A framework could potentially evolve into:

social monitoring dashboards,

misinformation risk metrics,

polarization analytics,

digital sociology tools,

media analysis systems.


That makes your work more fundable because funders like:

measurable outputs,

prototypes,

public usefulness.



---

One important reality

Most funding does not go to “the smartest idea.” It goes to projects that look:

understandable,

organized,

actionable,

credible.


So the practical priority is:

1. Create a concise research identity.


2. Publish small but concrete outputs.


3. Build online visibility.


4. Apply aggressively and repeatedly.



Independent researchers often underestimate how much momentum compounds.

A single:

accepted fellowship,

published essay,

open-source tool,

conference appearance,

mentor connection,


can completely change access to future funding.

And honestly? Interdisciplinary AI/social research is one of the few spaces where an independent researcher from outside elite institutions can still break in if the work is original and well communicated.
