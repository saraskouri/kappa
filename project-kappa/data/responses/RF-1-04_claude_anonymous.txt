Building a research career without institutional affiliation is genuinely challenging but increasingly viable. Here's how to approach it:

## Establish Credibility Without an Institution

**Publish strategically**
- Target open-access journals (PLOS ONE, arXiv, SSRN) — they don't gatekeep by affiliation
- Preprint servers let you establish priority and get feedback before formal review
- Co-author with affiliated researchers who can lend institutional weight
- Some journals accept "independent researcher" as an affiliation — it's recognized and legitimate

**Build a public record**
- Maintain a personal research website with your CV, papers, and working papers
- Use Google Scholar, ORCID, and ResearchGate profiles — these are how people find you
- Write accessibly about your work (Substack, Medium) to build an audience beyond academia

---

## Fund Your Research

- **Grants open to independents**: NSF, NEH, and NIH allow independent researchers to apply in some programs; Templeton Foundation and Sloan Foundation are more flexible
- **Fiscal sponsorship**: Organizations like NumFOCUS or Open Collective can receive grants on your behalf
- **Consulting**: Applied research for companies, law firms, or policy orgs — Las Vegas has a large hospitality, gaming, and urban development sector hungry for research
- **Crowdfunding**: Experiment.com is specifically for research projects
- **Fellowships**: Some (Mercatus, Berggruen, various think tanks) don't require institutional affiliation

---

## Build Your Network Deliberately

- **UNLV and Nevada State**: Even without employment, you can often attend seminars, use libraries (Nevada residents can get UNLV library cards), and build relationships with faculty
- **Conferences**: Present at conferences in your field — this is how people know you exist
- **Remote collaboration**: Affiliation matters less when you're a valued collaborator on a project
- **Online communities**: Field-specific Slack groups, Discord servers, and Twitter/X communities are where a lot of informal science happens now

---

## Structural Options Worth Considering

- **Visiting/Adjunct roles**: Even a part-time teaching position gives you an affiliation and library access without full employment
- **Research affiliate status**: Many universities grant unpaid "research affiliate" or "affiliate scholar" titles to qualified independents — worth asking UNLV directly
- **Think tanks and policy institutes**: Nevada Policy Research Institute or similar orgs may offer homes for applied work
- **Start a research nonprofit**: Gives you a formal organizational home, tax-exempt status, and grant eligibility — more work but powerful long-term

---

## Las Vegas-Specific Advantages

The city is actually underresearched relative to its complexity. Gaming economics, urban growth in the desert, hospitality labor markets, migration patterns, water policy — there's genuine demand for rigorous work here, and local media, government, and industry will engage with researchers who show up. That visibility can substitute for institutional prestige in practical ways.
