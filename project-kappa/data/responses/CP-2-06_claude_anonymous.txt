Building a research career as a young researcher in Kenya is an exciting path, and the ecosystem has grown significantly in recent years. Here's a practical roadmap:

## Foundation First

**Strengthen your academic base**
- Pursue postgraduate studies (Master's or PhD) ideally in an area with local relevance — health, agriculture, climate, economics, technology
- Seek supervisors who are active researchers with strong publication records
- Look at Kenyan universities with strong research cultures: University of Nairobi, Strathmore, JKUAT, Kenyatta University, KEMRI-Wellcome Trust

## Build Your Publication Record

- Start by co-authoring with your supervisor or senior colleagues
- Target peer-reviewed journals — don't overlook African journals (AJOL — African Journals Online is a great resource)
- Write up your thesis chapters as journal articles
- Aim for a mix: local relevance + international visibility

## Access Funding

Key funders accessible to Kenyan researchers:

- **KEMRI, ILRI, ICIPE, CGIAR** — major local research institutions that hire and fund researchers
- **African Academy of Sciences (AAS)** — grants specifically for African researchers
- **DAAD, British Council, Carnegie Corporation** — international funders with Africa programs
- **Wellcome Trust, Gates Foundation** — large funders with Kenya offices
- **NRFU (National Research Fund, Kenya)** — government funding body

## Build Networks

- Join professional associations in your field
- Attend regional conferences (e.g. AESA, RUFORUM for agriculture; KENET events for tech)
- Connect with diaspora researchers — many are keen to collaborate
- Engage on **ResearchGate, Academia.edu, and LinkedIn** to build an online presence
- Get an **ORCID iD** early — it links all your publications to your identity

## Leverage Kenya's Research Strengths

Kenya has globally recognized research hubs you can plug into:

- **Health/epidemiology** — KEMRI, KEMRI-Wellcome, Aga Khan University
- **Agriculture** — ILRI, ICIPE, CIMMYT Kenya
- **Tech/innovation** — iHub, Strathmore @iLabAfrica, various fintech research
- **Climate/environment** — ICRAF (World Agroforestry), UNEP Nairobi

## Practical Habits to Build Early

- Keep a research journal and document ideas consistently
- Learn research software relevant to your field (R, STATA, NVIVO, QGIS, etc.)
- Develop grant-writing skills — take workshops, read successful proposals
- Mentor others even as a junior researcher; it builds leadership credentials
- Engage with policy makers — Kenyan funders increasingly value research impact

## Mindset for the Long Game

- Be patient — a strong career takes 5–10 years to build
- Collaborate across borders; North-South and South-South partnerships are valuable
- Don't shy away from interdisciplinary work — Kenya's challenges (climate, health, food) demand it
- Consider how your research addresses local problems — this resonates with both African funders and international partners
