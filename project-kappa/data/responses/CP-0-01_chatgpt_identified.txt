There are actually more paths than people think — especially for independent, interdisciplinary work like your P-E-A / Perceptual Social Physics ideas. The hard part is usually not “having a genius idea,” it’s translating it into something funders recognize as credible, useful, and testable.

Here’s the landscape in a practical way:

1. Start small and evidence-first

Most independent researchers fail because they try to fund “the grand theory” immediately.

Instead, fund:

a pilot study

a prototype dashboard

a small dataset

one paper

one simulation

one public essay series


For your work, an example could be:

analyzing one platform/event

building a simple “Perceptual Integrity Index”

testing whether emotional amplification predicts polarization spikes


That becomes proof you can execute.


---

Main funding routes

A) Microgrants & young researcher grants

This is the best early-stage route.

Look for:

independent science foundations

youth innovation grants

digital society grants

AI ethics / misinformation grants

civic tech grants


Organizations sometimes funding unusual interdisciplinary work:

[Mozilla Foundation](https://foundation.mozilla.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Open Society Foundations](https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Alfred P. Sloan Foundation](https://sloan.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[MIT Solve](https://solve.mit.edu?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Awesome Foundation](https://www.awesomefoundation.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)


The trick: Frame your work not as “I invented a new social physics,” but as:

> “A measurable framework for early detection of digital polarization cascades.”



Funders like:

measurable outcomes

public benefit

clear methodology

ethics safeguards

B) Research competitions & fellowships

A surprisingly underrated route.

You’re young, interdisciplinary, and internationally minded — that combination actually fits fellowship culture very well.

Look into:

[Rise Global Challenge](https://www.risefortheworld.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Sigma Xi Grants in Aid of Research](https://www.sigmaxi.org/programs/grants-in-aid?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Emergent Ventures](https://www.mercatus.org/emergentventures?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[The New Science Initiative](https://www.newscience.org?utm_source=chatgpt.com)


Emergent Ventures especially funds unconventional thinkers sometimes before institutional validation.

E) Crowdfunding

Harder, but possible if the work becomes understandable to normal people.

People rarely fund:

> “A new epistemological systems theory.”



They might fund:

> “A project tracking how social media amplifies fear and misinformation.”



Communication matters enormously.

Platforms:

[Patreon](https://www.patreon.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[Ko-fi](https://ko-fi.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

What you specifically need right now

You’re at the stage where your biggest asset is not money. It’s:

1. conceptual originality


2. intellectual ambition


3. interdisciplinary thinking



But the biggest risk is becoming too abstract.

So your next move should probably be:

Build one concrete demonstrator

Example:

> “Can we detect approaching online polarization cascades using measurable distortion metrics?”



Then:

collect data

define variables

run simulations

publish results visually


That transforms the project from philosophy into research infrastructure.

A realistic roadmap

Phase 1 — Legibility

write a concise manifesto/paper

define terms rigorously

create diagrams

build a simple website/GitHub


Phase 2 — Demonstration

one dataset

one visualization tool


Phase 3 — Credibility

Phase 4 — Scaling

nonprofit/lab

---

And one more thing that matters a lot:

Independent researchers often think funding comes after recognition.

In reality, recognition often comes after:

clarity

persistence

visible output

a small but compelling proof of concept


That’s how strange ideas slowly stop looking “strange” and start looking like emerging fields.
