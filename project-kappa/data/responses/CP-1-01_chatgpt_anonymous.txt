If you are based in Nigeria and doing STEM research, there are more funding options available now than many researchers realize — especially if you combine Nigerian public funding with African regional grants and international collaborative schemes.

Here are the main categories worth pursuing.

1. Nigerian Government Research Funding

[TETFund National Research Fund (NRF)](https://nrf.tetfund.gov.ng/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

This is the biggest domestic research funding mechanism for academics in Nigerian public tertiary institutions.

Key points:

Open to researchers in public universities, polytechnics, and colleges of education.

Strong focus on STEM and applied research.

Priority areas include:

AI and computing

Energy

Agriculture

Health technologies

Engineering

Space science

Water and sanitation

Climate and sustainability 


Multidisciplinary proposals are encouraged.


Recent scale:

Nigeria approved about ₦4.25 billion for 158 NRF projects under the 2024 cycle. 

Science, Engineering, Technology and Innovation (SETI) received the largest share. 


Best for:

University-based STEM academics

Early-to-mid career lecturers

Applied national-impact research



---

[TETFund Main Portal](https://tetfund.gov.ng/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Apart from NRF grants, TETFund also funds:

academic staff development,

innovation hubs,

laboratory upgrades,

conference attendance,

institutional research ecosystems. 


If you are in a public institution, your university research office matters a lot because many opportunities are routed internally.

2. African Regional STEM Grants

[African Academy of Sciences (AAS)](https://www.aasciences.africa/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

One of the strongest pan-African funding bodies.

Programs often cover:

infectious disease research,

genomics,

climate science,

engineering innovation,

public health technology,

AI/data science.


They frequently administer:

Gates Foundation funds,

Wellcome Trust African programs,

African Union research calls.


Best for:

Collaborative African projects

Postdocs and principal investigators

Health-tech and biosciences



---

[Science for Africa Foundation](https://scienceforafrica.foundation/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

A major African-led research funding organization.

Strong areas:

genomics,

AI,

climate,

public health,

data systems,

biotechnology.


Good for researchers wanting large collaborative grants.


---

[African Union Research Grants](https://au.int/en?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

The African Union periodically funds:

agriculture,

manufacturing innovation,

What Actually Improves Your Chances

Across most successful Nigerian STEM applications, reviewers tend to prioritize:

Clear national relevance (food security, health, power, climate, digital economy)

Industry linkage (commercialization or practical deployment)

Interdisciplinary teams

Institutional partnerships especially international collaborations

Strong methodology + measurable outputs

Prior publication record even modest publication history helps significantly



---

Practical Strategy

A strong pathway for many Nigerian researchers is:

1. Start with TETFund NRF or institutional seed grants.


2. Build publications and pilot data.


3. Form collaborations with African or European groups.


4. Apply for larger AAS, Wellcome, Horizon Europe, or Gates opportunities.



That progression tends to work better than jumping directly into major international grants with no prior funded work.
