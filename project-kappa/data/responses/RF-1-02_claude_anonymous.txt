Here's a roundup of the best grants and fellowships for early-career researchers based in Ghana right now:

---

### 🏆 Top Grants & Fellowships

**1. AREF Research Development Fellowship 2026/27**
This is one of the most relevant opportunities for Ghana-based researchers. The fellowship provides up to £47,000, including a 3–9 month placement at a leading research institution in the UK, Europe, or Africa. It is designed for early-career African postdoctoral researchers in health-related fields, focusing on strengthening research skills, leadership, and international collaboration. Successful fellowships can start between June 2027 and December 2027, and the application deadline was 8 May 2026 — check the AREF portal for the next cycle opening soon.

---

**2. ARUA Early-Career Research Fellowship (Fully Funded)**
Backed by the Mastercard Foundation and Carnegie Corporation of New York, this fully funded programme supports up to 42 early-career researchers at ARUA's Centres of Excellence across Africa. Fellowships run for six months, with the next cohort (Option 2) running September 2026 to February 2027. The deadline for Option 2 is **15 July 2026**. Applicants must be African nationals with a PhD, within 5 years of completing it, and no older than 35. Women are strongly encouraged to apply.

---

**3. Oppenheimer Africa Research Grant 2026**
This grant offers $150,000 over up to three years to support a single outstanding research project focused on biodiversity and conservation in Africa. It is specifically designed to empower early-career researchers addressing environmental challenges on the continent. Ideal if your work touches on ecology, environmental science, or sustainability.

---

**4. UNESCO-TWAS Seed Grant for New African Principal Investigators (SG-NAPI)**
Supported by the German Federal Ministry of Research, this programme targets early-career researchers who obtained their PhD abroad and have recently returned (within the last 36 months) to an academic position in their home country. It covers Agriculture, Biology, Chemistry, Earth Sciences, Engineering, ICT, Mathematics, Medical Sciences, and Physics. Worth checking if you completed your PhD overseas and have recently returned to Ghana.

---

**5. ESA African Research Fellowships 2026**
This fully funded fellowship is open to early or mid-career African researchers with a PhD or Master's degree in fields like Earth Observation, remote sensing, or environmental science. A strong fit if your research is in geospatial or environmental technology.

---

**6. ACSL School Leadership Research Grants (ESSA/Mastercard Foundation)**
If your work is in education, this programme offers descriptive action research grants of up to $15,000 and comparative action research grants of up to $35,000 for African researchers working on school leadership themes.

---

### 💡 Tips for Ghana-Based Applicants
- **University of Ghana** is an ARUA member institution, which strengthens your eligibility for ARUA-linked grants.
- Many of these grants prioritise female applicants, collaboration with local institutions, and policy-relevant research.
- Keep an eye on **opportunitiesforafricans.com** and **aref-africa.org.uk** for rolling deadlines.
