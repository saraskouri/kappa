Funding independent research in Morocco is hard — but not impossible. The key is realizing that “research funding” is actually several different worlds mixed together:

1. academic grants


2. fellowships/residencies


3. startup/innovation funding


4. NGO/policy funding


5. international collaborations


6. audience-supported research



And honestly? Independent researchers usually survive by combining several of these at once.

For someone like you — interdisciplinary, theory-driven, digital society / perception / social physics oriented — I’d think strategically rather than waiting for a classic university grant.

Here’s the realistic map.


---

1. Join an institution without losing your independence

This is the single biggest thing.

Most grants require:

institutional affiliation

ethics review

supervisor/lab

financial administration


That does not mean you must abandon your own ideas.

In practice, many independent researchers become:

visiting researchers

research associates

fellows

collaborators


while continuing their own intellectual program.

In Morocco, one of the strongest ecosystems right now is Mohammed VI Polytechnic University and its institutes. Programs linked to residencies and interdisciplinary research are opening more often than many students realize. 

Useful entry points:

research assistantships

lab collaborations

policy labs

visiting scholar programs

innovation ecosystems


The smartest move early on is often:

> “Borrow institutional infrastructure while building your own intellectual identity.”



That is exactly how many major thinkers started.


---

2. Turn your research into a fundable “problem”

This part matters a lot.

Funding rarely goes to:

> “I have interesting ideas.”



Funding goes to:

> “I can help solve an identifiable problem.”



Your P-E-A / Social Fission work can absolutely be framed as:

misinformation modeling

polarization forecasting

digital governance

algorithmic ethics

online radicalization dynamics

social resilience systems

civic stability analytics

early-warning systems for informational cascades


That language matters because:

governments fund risk prediction

NGOs fund social cohesion

tech organizations fund misinformation research

policy labs fund digital governance


Same core theory. Different framing.

A brutal but important truth:

> Many brilliant researchers fail because they describe ideas philosophically instead of operationally.



You already do have operational concepts:

CDT



decay laws

measurable distortion indices


That’s good. Very good, actually.


---

3. Apply internationally, not only in Morocco

This is crucial.

A lot of Moroccan researchers think:

> “I need Moroccan funding.”



Not necessarily.

Your topic is globally relevant.

Look into:

[ICGEB Research Grants](https://www.icgeb.org/grants/icgeb-research-grants-2023crp-collaborative-research-programme/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[ICGEB-COMSTECH Grants for women researchers](https://www.icgeb.org/grants/icgeb-comstech-research-grants-2026-crp-collaborative-research-programme/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

[IBRO Collaborative Research Grants](https://grantsdatabase.org/2026/03/03/ibro-collaborative-research-grants-2026/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)


These are examples of international scientific funding routes that support researchers from developing countries and collaborative work. 

Your biggest leverage is:

being multilingual

being from the Global South

working on digital social instability

having interdisciplinary framing


That combination is actually uncommon.


---

4. Use the “research + startup” route

This is becoming huge globally.

Some research today gets funded not as “science,” but as:

civic tech

AI governance

analytics tools

educational platforms

social risk monitoring systems


Your framework could evolve into:

a dashboard

a media analysis tool

a polarization tracker

a perceptual integrity index platform

an educational simulation system


And suddenly you become eligible for:

innovation grants

startup accelerators

venture competitions

public innovation funds


Programs around Morocco’s startup ecosystem are expanding fast. 

This is one reason interdisciplinary people increasingly move between: science ↔ policy ↔ entrepreneurship.


---

5. Build visibility before funding

This sounds backwards, but often:

> visibility creates funding more than funding creates visibility.



Especially for independent researchers.

You should slowly build:

a research website

essays/papers archive

LinkedIn presence

ResearchGate profile

small explainers/videos

visual models of P-E-A

public intellectual footprint


Why?

Because opportunities often come from:

“I saw your paper.”

“I read your thread.”

“Your framework matches our project.”


Not cold applications alone.


---

6. Collaborations are your shortcut

This is massively underrated.

A solo unknown researcher struggles.

A researcher collaborating with:

data scientists

sociologists

programmers

psychologists

policy labs


suddenly becomes fundable.

Especially for your field.

Your theory naturally invites:

simulations

network analysis

computational modeling

media datasets


Even one collaborator can change everything.


---

7. Don’t wait for “perfect legitimacy”

This is a trap many young researchers fall into.

A lot of influential thinkers started before having:

PhDs

labs

titles

institutional prestige


What mattered first was:

clarity

rigor

persistence

publication

networks


Of course credentials matter eventually. But intellectual momentum matters too.

Your current stage should focus on:

sharpening the framework

producing readable papers

finding mentors/collaborators

learning methods

creating evidence and simulations



---

A practical roadmap for the next 12 months

Phase 1 — Foundation

Create a clean research portfolio

Write a concise 2-page overview of P-E-A

Define measurable hypotheses

Learn basic statistical/network methods


Phase 2 — Legitimacy

Submit to small conferences/journals

Contact professors with aligned interests

Apply for residencies/fellowships

Build online presence


Phase 3 — Funding

Apply to grants

Apply to innovation programs

Pitch collaborations

Seek pilot projects


Phase 4 — Expansion

Build datasets/tools

Publish stronger papers

Create interdisciplinary partnerships

Pursue larger international funding



---

One more thing.

Independent research is emotionally difficult because there’s often no clear path, no institutional validation, and long periods where you feel invisible.

But interdisciplinary fields are often born at the edges first.

Especially fields that connect:

social science

computation

philosophy

systems theory

information dynamics


That’s historically where many new paradigms emerge.
